        amount: 1
        unit: can (6 oz)

        unit: cans (15 oz each)

    items:
      - item: large bell pepper

        unit: whole

      - item: small onion
        amount: 1
        unit: whole

  - group: Seasoning

        amount: 1

      - item: dried basil

        amount: 1

        amount: 1
        unit: teaspoon
        note: plus more to taste
      - item: black pepper
        amount: 1
        unit: teaspoon
        note: plus more to taste
      - item: sugar
        amount: 1
        unit: teaspoon
        note: to balance acidity
      - item: olive oil
        amount: 2
        unit: tablespoons
instructions:
  - step: Heat olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
  - step: Add diced onion and bell pepper and sauté for 5–7 minutes until softened.
  - step: Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  - step: Add the tomato paste and stir constantly for 2 minutes to caramelize slightly.
  - step: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato sauce, stirring to combine.
  - step: Add oregano, basil, Italian seasoning, salt, pepper, and sugar. Stir well.
  - step: Bring the sauce to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low.
  - step: Cover partially and simmer for 1 to 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally.
  - step: Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed before serving.
notes: |
  Tips for the perfect family spaghetti sauce:
  - Let the sauce simmer low and slow for the best depth of flavor
  - Taste and adjust seasoning near the end of cooking
  - The sauce freezes beautifully in quart-sized containers for up to 3 months
  - For a meat sauce, brown 1 lb ground beef or Italian sausage before adding the tomatoes
  - A small pinch of red pepper flakes adds a nice background warmth
