---
layout: recipe
title: Pork Butt
subtitle: Texas Style Low and Slow
category: smoked
prep_time: 30 minutes
cook_time: 12-14 hours
servings: 12-15
images:
  - path: assets/img/portfolio/pork-butt.jpg
    alt: Texas Style Smoked Pork Butt
ingredients:
  - group: Rub
    items:
      - item: kosher salt
        amount: 2
        unit: tablespoons
      - item: coarse black pepper
        amount: 2
        unit: tablespoons
      - item: paprika
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
      - item: garlic powder
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
      - item: onion powder
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
      - item: brown sugar
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
      - item: cayenne pepper
        amount: 1
        unit: teaspoon
        note: optional, for heat
  - group: Main
    items:
      - item: bone-in pork butt
        amount: 8-10
        unit: pounds
        note: also called pork shoulder
      - item: yellow mustard
        amount: 2
        unit: tablespoons
        note: as binder
      - item: apple cider vinegar
        note: for spritzing
      - item: wood chunks
        note: hickory, oak, or pecan
instructions:
  - group: Preparation
    steps:
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Trim excess fat cap to about 1/4 inch thickness"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Apply thin layer of yellow mustard as a binder"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Mix all rub ingredients and apply generously to all sides"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Let the meat sit at room temperature for 30-45 minutes"
  - group: Smoking
    steps:
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Preheat smoker to 225-250°F"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Add wood chunks for smoke"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Place pork butt fat side up on the grate"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Smoke for 8-10 hours, spritzing with apple cider vinegar every 2 hours after the first 4 hours"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] When internal temp reaches 165°F, wrap in butcher paper if desired (Texas crutch)"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Continue cooking until internal temp reaches 195-205°F and probe slides in like butter"
  - group: Rest and Serve
    steps:
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Rest in a cooler wrapped in towels for 1-2 hours"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Pull the pork apart with forks or bear claws"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Mix in some bark pieces throughout"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Serve on buns or alongside your favorite sides"
notes: "[PLACEHOLDER] Ricky will add personal notes and tips here. Key temps: 165°F for wrapping decision, 195-205°F for pull point. The stall around 150-170°F is normal - be patient."
equipment:
  - Smoker or Big Green Egg
  - Thermapen or meat thermometer
  - Spray bottle
  - Butcher paper (optional)
---
