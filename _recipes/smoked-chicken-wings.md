title: Smoked Chicken Wings

category: smoked

cook_time: 2-2.5 hours
servings: 4-6
images:
  - path: assets/img/portfolio/smoked-chicken-wings.jpg
    alt: Smoked Chicken Wings
ingredients:
  - group: Rub
    items:
      - item: paprika
        amount: 2
        unit: tablespoons
      - item: garlic powder
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
      - item: onion powder
        amount: 1
        unit: teaspoon
      - item: chili powder
        amount: 1
        unit: teaspoon
      - item: kosher salt
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
      - item: black pepper
        amount: 1
        unit: teaspoon
      - item: brown sugar
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
      - item: cayenne pepper
        amount: 1/2
        unit: teaspoon
        note: optional, for extra heat
  - group: Main
    items:
      - item: chicken wings
        amount: 3-4
        unit: pounds
        note: tips removed, flats and drums separated
      - item: baking powder
        amount: 1
        unit: tablespoon
        note: for crispy skin
      - item: olive oil
        amount: 2
        unit: tablespoons
        note: light coating
  - group: Sauce (optional)
    items:
      - item: butter
        amount: 4
        unit: tablespoons
        note: melted
      - item: hot sauce
        amount: 1/2
        unit: cup
        note: Frank's RedHot or similar
      - item: honey
        amount: 2
        unit: tablespoons
instructions:
  - group: Preparation
    steps:
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Pat wings completely dry with paper towels — this is critical for crispy skin"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Toss wings with baking powder and olive oil in a large bowl"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Mix all rub ingredients and season wings generously on all sides"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Place wings uncovered on a rack in the refrigerator for 1 hour if time allows (improves crispiness)"
  - group: Smoking
    steps:
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Preheat smoker to 225°F with fruit wood (apple or cherry)"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Place wings directly on the grate in a single layer, not touching"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Smoke for 1.5 hours at 225°F until skin begins to set"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Increase temperature to 350-375°F for the final 30-45 minutes to crisp the skin"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Wings are done when internal temperature reaches 175-180°F at the thickest part"
  - group: Finishing
    steps:
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] For sauced wings: toss in butter/hot sauce/honey mixture while hot"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] For dry wings: serve as-is with the rub — the bark is incredible"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Let rest 5 minutes before serving"
      - step: "[PLACEHOLDER] Serve with ranch or blue cheese dressing and celery sticks"
notes: "[PLACEHOLDER] Ricky will add personal notes here. The key to crispy smoked wings is dry skin — pat them dry and the baking powder trick works wonders. The two-temp method (low then high) gives you smoke flavor AND crispy skin."
equipment:
  - Smoker or Big Green Egg
  - Thermapen or meat thermometer
  - Wire rack and sheet pan
