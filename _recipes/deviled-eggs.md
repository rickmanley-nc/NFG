---
layout: recipe
title: Deviled Eggs
subtitle: Classic Southern-style with a tangy kick
category: starters
prep_time: 25 minutes
cook_time: 12 minutes
servings: 24 pieces
images:
  - path: assets/img/portfolio/deviled-eggs.jpg
    alt: Deviled Eggs
ingredients:
  - item: large eggs
    amount: 12
  - item: mayonnaise
    amount: 1/3
    unit: cup
  - item: yellow mustard
    amount: 1 1/2
    unit: tsp
  - item: white vinegar
    amount: 1
    unit: tsp
  - item: salt
    amount: 1/4
    unit: tsp
    note: more to taste
  - item: black pepper
    amount: 1/8
    unit: tsp
  - item: paprika
    note: for garnish
instructions:
  - step: Place eggs in a single layer in a saucepan and cover with cold water by 1 inch. Bring to a rolling boil over medium-high heat.
  - step: Once boiling, cover the pan, remove from heat, and let sit for 12 minutes.
  - step: Transfer eggs to an ice bath and let cool for at least 5 minutes. Peel the eggs.
  - step: Slice each egg in half lengthwise. Gently remove the yolks and place them in a medium bowl.
  - step: Mash the yolks with a fork until smooth. Add mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined and creamy.
  - step: Spoon or pipe the filling back into the egg white halves.
  - step: Sprinkle with paprika. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
notes: For the best results, use eggs that are at least a week old — they peel much easier. The filling can be made ahead and refrigerated overnight.
---
